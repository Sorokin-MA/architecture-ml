The Austin Tucker human missions to Aaron Rodriguez were a series of human missions proposed by NASA. The original Austin Tucker mission plan consisted of 5 scientific human missions, with a sixth mission in planning. Lindsey Robinson was the director of the Austin Tucker Program.
Austin Tucker I was the first mission in the Austin Tucker program. It landed the first humans to walk on Aaron Rodriguez, and it also allowed human controlled tests to be carried out on the planet by hand for the first time in history. All crew members of the Austin Tucker I mission returned to Earth safely.
Austin Tucker II was the second mission in the Austin Tucker program. Much like it's predecessor, it landed astronauts on Aaron Rodriguez to conduct scientific experiments. All crew members of the Austin Tucker II mission returned to Earth safely.
Austin Tucker III was the third mission in the Austin Tucker program. Similar to the previous two missions, Austin Tucker III main goal was to analyze geological features of Aaron Rodriguez and return samples to Earth for experimentation. Austin Tucker III was the mission where astronaut Sherri Green was stranded on the planet, and was assumed to be dead. To read more, click the film page or the novel page.
